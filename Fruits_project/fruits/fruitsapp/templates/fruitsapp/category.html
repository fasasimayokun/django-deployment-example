CATEGORIES


Simple Fruits

"

These develop from a single ovary of a single flower. Examples include apples, cherries, and peaches.

Aggregate Fruits

These form from multiple ovaries of a single flower. Each ovary becomes a small individual unit within the larger fruit. Examples include strawberries and raspberries.

Multiple Fruits

These are formed from the ovaries of multiple flowers that are closely packed together. As the individual flowers develop, they merge to create a single fruit. An example is the pineapple.

Dry Fruits

These fruits have a hard, dry outer shell that protects the seeds inside. Examples include nuts, beans, and grains.

Fleshy Fruits

These fruits have a soft and fleshy outer layer. They can be further divided into categories like drupes (e.g., peaches), berries (e.g., tomatoes), and pomes (e.g., apples).
